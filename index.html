About — My Learning Journey

Foundations — apron on (basics & protection)

Sixteen years in India shaped my work ethic; the UK broadened my options. I sampled subjects, found I genuinely liked economics, and took Economics, Maths, Physics at A‑level. Now I’m studying B.Sc. Economics at the University of Birmingham, turning analysis into action.

Early Steps — learning the grip (first practice)

To get beyond theory, I joined the FinTech Society: a five‑person team building Python strategies, back‑testing across regimes, and presenting under scrutiny. Data stopped being abstract and became something I could shape, explain, and defend.

Exploration — sharpening the blade (real‑world context)

J.P. Morgan & HSBC virtual experiences: screened 5 M&A targets, valued a £803M company with DCF & comps, stress‑tested a 110.8% premium and 15% supply‑chain risk, and read DCM conditions. It sharpened my sense for what stakeholders actually need in a model and a memo.

Consulting View — finding the cut (prep & method)

At Oxbridge Analytics I synthesised energy‑market research across 10+ engagements into decision briefs, cleaned/structured large datasets, and supported changes linked to ~20% emissions reduction. It taught me why clean data is non‑negotiable.

Impact — ten even slices (repeatable outcomes)

At Nationwide Building Society: reconciled multi‑system data into Power BI to make month‑end packs ~12% faster and more accurate; raised completeness to ~97% across 400+ records (cutting rework ~20%) and added an Excel self‑check so quality persists.

Community — eyes‑up rhythm (confidence from a strong team)

Joined Young Asians in Finance to plan high‑quality events, produce accurate materials, and uphold confidentiality while learning from professionals ahead of me. I’m building my network by contributing value.

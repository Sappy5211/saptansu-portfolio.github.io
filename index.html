About — My Learning Journey

Foundations — apron on (basics & protection)

Sixteen years in India shaped my work ethic; the UK broadened my options. I sampled subjects, found I genuinely liked economics, and took Economics, Maths, Physics at A-level. Now I’m studying B.Sc. Economics at the University of Birmingham, turning analysis into action.

Early Steps — learning the grip (first practice)

To get beyond theory, I joined the FinTech Society: a five-person team building Python strategies, back-testing across regimes, and presenting under scrutiny. It’s where data stopped being abstract and became something I could shape, explain, and defend.

Exploration — sharpening the blade (real-world context)

Student projects weren’t enough, so I completed J.P. Morgan & HSBC virtual experiences: screened 5 M&A targets, valued a £803M company with DCF & comps, stress-tested a 110.8% premium and 15% supply-chain risk, and read DCM conditions (early-2021 issuance ~66% BBB+/-). It sharpened my judgement on what stakeholders actually need in a model and a memo.

Consulting View — finding the cut (prep & method)

Curious about consulting, I volunteered at Oxbridge Analytics on sustainability work: synthesised energy-market research across 10+ engagements into decision briefs, cleaned/structured large datasets, and supported changes linked to ~20% emissions reduction. It taught me why clean data is non-negotiable.

Impact — ten even slices (repeatable outcomes)

My north star was finance, so I earned a Nationwide Building Society internship and focused on reporting quality and controls.

I reconciled multi-system data into Power BI and made month-end packs ~12% faster and more accurate; lifted data completeness to ~97% across 400+ records (cutting rework ~20%) and built an Excel self-check so the process stays tight next time..

I also led a DEI/early-careers proposal rated best by leadership and briefed senior leaders on Microsoft Copilot with a controls lens.

Community — eyes-up rhythm (confidence from a strong team)

Coffee chats kept surfacing the same advice: build a network, practise leadership, collaborate well. That’s why I joined Young Asians in Finance—to plan and deliver high-quality events, produce accurate materials, and uphold confidentiality while learning from people ahead of me. I’m building my network by contributing value.

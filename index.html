About — My Learning Journey

Mise en place (how it started)

I grew up in India for sixteen years, in a system where “success” often meant one path: science. Five years ago I moved to the UK and discovered a different landscape — one where finance, economics, and analytics could all be a career. I’ve always liked maths, patterns, and problem-solving, so I chose Economics and took Maths & Physics at A-level. I’m now reading B.Sc. Economics at the University of Birmingham, building a toolkit that turns raw data into clear decisions.

Starter (finding my crew)

To figure out the roles behind “finance,” I joined communities and learned by doing. In the FinTech Society, I built and back-tested Python strategies with a five-person team. That’s where data stopped being abstract; it became something I could shape, explain, and defend.

Tasting flight (exploring the field)

2I wanted breadth, so I completed J.P. Morgan & HSBC virtual experiences. I screened M&A targets, valued a £803M company with DCF & comps, stress-tested assumptions (e.g., 110.8% bid premium, 15% supply-chain risk), and analysed DCM conditions to understand funding choices. It sharpened my presentation skills and my sense for what stakeholders actually need.

Side dish (consulting perspective)

I then contributed at Oxbridge Analytics on sustainability-focused work: synthesising energy-market research across 10+ engagements, cleaning and structuring large datasets, and helping teams turn research into actionable briefs. It taught me delivery discipline and the value of clean data for planning and forecasting.

Main course (real business impact)

As a Finance Intern at Nationwide Building Society, I worked on reporting quality and controls:

Improved month-end pack accuracy and cycle time by ~12% using Power BI (Power Query) and advanced Excel.

Drove data completeness to ~97% across 400+ records, reducing rework by ~20%.

Corrected 60+ board-pack entries and built an Excel self-check to standardise updates and strengthen governance. Beyond the numbers, I led a small team on a DEI/early-careers proposal that leadership rated best and briefed senior leaders on Microsoft Copilot use-cases with a risk/controls lens.

Pass the plate (community & network)

Great teams accelerate great work. That’s why I joined Young Asians in Finance — to help organise high-quality events for early-career talent and to keep learning from people ahead of me. I’m building my network the right way: by contributing, communicating clearly, and holding a high bar for accuracy and professionalism.
